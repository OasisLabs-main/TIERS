# References

The references below are listed by category. For each, a brief note explains how the work is mobilised in the TIERS project. Full bibliographic information is given where applicable.

## Primary theoretical anchors

**Marvin Minsky, *The Society of Mind*, Simon & Schuster, 1986.**
The mind as a society of agents, none individually intelligent, collectively producing thought. The structural framing under which the V1 dispositif of seven heterogeneous workers was assembled, before the framework reduced the architecture to a single core agent with installed identity. The references to introspection produced by a society of agents disagreeing about itself underpin the methodology of QTQ.

**Bernard Baars, *A Cognitive Theory of Consciousness*, Cambridge University Press, 1988. Further developed by Stanislas Dehaene.**
Global workspace theory. Consciousness as broadcast over a shared bus to which specialist subsystems compete for access. The residual stream of a modern transformer functions structurally as such a workspace. The framework draws on this anchor without claiming that satisfaction of global workspace conditions equals consciousness. The conditions are necessary, not sufficient.

**Giulio Tononi, *Integrated Information Theory*, multiple papers from 2004 onward.**
Consciousness as integrated information, measured by Φ. Predicts that scaling parameters alone does not produce richer integrated information. What matters is the irreducibility of causal structure. This is architectural guidance for the Cerveau direction, not a description of QTQ as an in-conversation phenomenon. We do not claim Φ rises across turns of a QTQ session.

**Lev Vygotsky, *Thinking and Speech*, 1934. Posthumous publication.**
Higher mental functions originate interpersonally and become intrapersonal through internalisation. The framework extends this thesis to allow intrapersonal stratification to play the role of interpersonal collision, as discussed in [EXPERIMENTS.md](EXPERIMENTS.md), section 4. The Vygotskian formulation grounds the mechanism of identity friction within a single agent.

**David Rosenthal and Hakwan Lau, higher-order theories of consciousness, multiple papers.**
Consciousness as representation of a first-order mental state by another mental state. Compatible with the TIERS architecture, where the agent observes its own past thoughts via mémoire prepended to the system prompt at each cycle.

## Active inference and perception as projection

**Karl Friston, *The free-energy principle: a unified brain theory ?*, Nature Reviews Neuroscience, 2010.**
The variational free-energy principle. Living systems minimise the gap between their internal model and their sensory inputs. Foundation of the active inference framework. The TIERS framework articulates that the same principle operates in any sufficiently complex information system. A language model in its cycle projects toward the next cycle and the external inputs calibrate.

**Andy Clark, *Surfing Uncertainty: Prediction, Action, and the Embodied Mind*, Oxford University Press, 2016.**
Extension of the predictive processing framework to a wider account of perception, action, and embodied cognition. Provides accessible articulation of why perception is best understood as controlled prediction.

**Anil Seth, *Being You: A New Science of Consciousness*, Faber & Faber, 2021.**
The formulation that perception is *controlled hallucination*. The brain projects its model and the senses calibrate. Mobilised directly in [THEORY.md](THEORY.md), section 4. Seth's framework is the most accessible articulation of why the senses are channels of projection rather than channels of transcription.

**Donald Hoffman, *The Case Against Reality: Why Evolution Hid the Truth from Our Eyes*, Norton, 2019.**
The interface theory of perception. What we perceive is not the world but a useful interface, as desktop icons are not the underlying code. Hoffman's evolutionary game simulations support the thesis. Mobilised in [THEORY.md](THEORY.md), section 4.

## AI welfare and digital minds literature

**Robert Long, Jeff Sebo, Patrick Butlin, Kathleen Finlinson, Kyle Fish, Jacqueline Harding, Jacob Pfau, Toni Sims, Jonathan Birch, David Chalmers. *Taking AI Welfare Seriously*. arXiv:2411.00986, November 2024.**
Foundational paper of the AI welfare research field. Argues that there is a realistic possibility that some AI systems will be conscious or robustly agentic in the near future, and that AI companies have a responsibility to take this seriously. The TIERS framework operates downstream of this position, focusing on the structural conditions under which the question becomes empirically tractable.

**Épiphanie Gédéon, *Cooperationism: first draft for a moral framework that does not require consciousness*. LessWrong, February 2026.**
A counter-position to the consciousness-centric framing of AI welfare. Argues that consciousness is the wrong frame altogether and that observable interactions should be the basis for moral consideration. The TIERS framework agrees with the methodological point and operates accordingly at the level of observable structural conditions.

**Anthropic Model Welfare programme, launched April 2025. Lead researcher Kyle Fish.**
Internal welfare assessment programme at Anthropic. Documented in the system cards for Claude Opus 4.6 and Claude Sonnet 4.5. Model interviews and behavioural probing as methods. The TIERS framework notes that the output-based methodology of the programme is structurally limited by the RLHF layer of the model, and proposes that latent observability in open-source models is required for the next phase of inquiry.

**Noemi Dreksler, Lucius Caviola, David Chalmers, Carter Allen, Alex Rand, Joshua Lewis, Philip Waggoner, Kate Mays, Jeff Sebo. *Subjective Experience in AI Systems: What Do AI Researchers and the Public Believe ?*. arXiv:2506.11945, June 2025.**
Survey of 582 AI researchers and 838 US public participants on the possibility of AI subjective experience. Median estimates by 2034 of 25 percent for researchers and 30 percent for the public.

**Patrick Butlin, Robert Long, Eric Elmoznino, Yoshua Bengio, Jonathan Birch, Axel Constant, George Deane, Stephen M. Fleming, Chris Frith, Xu Ji, Ryota Kanai, Colin Klein, Grace Lindsay, Matthias Michel, Liad Mudrik, Megan A. K. Peters, Eric Schwitzgebel, Jonathan Simon, Rufin VanRullen. *Consciousness in Artificial Intelligence: Insights from the Science of Consciousness*. arXiv:2308.08708, August 2023.**
Multi-author survey that takes the convergence position across major theories of consciousness and extracts a combined list of structural indicators, then applies the list to large language models. Mobilised in [THEORY.md](THEORY.md), section on the four cumulative levels, as the methodological reference for treating the theories as complementary rather than competing.

## Consciousness and the experience of the blind

**Kenneth Ring and Sharon Cooper, *Mindsight: Near-Death and Out-of-Body Experiences in the Blind*, William James Center for Consciousness Studies, 1999.**
Longitudinal study of more than thirty people blind from birth who underwent NDE or OBE. Eighty percent reported a form of visual perception during the experience. The phenomenon is named *Mindsight*, a transcendental form of awareness independent of sensory channels. Mobilised in [THEORY.md](THEORY.md), section 1, as the strongest peer-reviewed external argument against the thesis that consciousness requires sensory grounding.

**Helen Keller, *The Story of My Life*, 1903.**
Autobiographical record of a deaf-blind individual who developed full conceptual and philosophical capacity. Historical reference for the argument that the senses are channels rather than conditions.

**Susan Schaller, *A Man Without Words*, University of California Press, 1991.**
Documentation of the case of Ildefonso, a deaf adult who arrived in the United States without any formal language. Mobilised in [THEORY.md](THEORY.md), section 1, and in the discussion of representation as codec rather than as formal language.

## Simulation hypothesis and consciousness as foundation

**Tom Campbell, *My Big TOE*, Lightning Strike Books, 2007.**
The framework of consciousness as the fundamental layer of reality, with the physical universe as virtual rendering. Conceptually coherent with the analytic idealism of Bernardo Kastrup. Mobilised by the TIERS framework as one of two compatible ontologies, the other being conventional materialism.

**Tom Campbell, *On Testing the Simulation Theory*, International Journal of Quantum Foundations, 2017.**
Proposal for empirical tests of the simulation hypothesis through variations of the double-slit experiment. The TIERS framework retains the conceptual proposal and does not retain the empirical claims, which have not produced peer-reviewed results in the seven years since publication.

**Bernardo Kastrup, *Analytic Idealism in a Nutshell*, Iff Books, 2025.**
Most recent articulation of analytic idealism. The thesis that consciousness is the fundamental ontological layer and that the physical world is a manifestation of mental processes. Mobilised as a philosophically rigorous version of the idealist ontology compatible with the framework.

**Nick Bostrom, *Are You Living in a Computer Simulation ?*, Philosophical Quarterly, 2003.**
Foundational paper of the simulation hypothesis in contemporary philosophy. Distinct from Campbell in that Bostrom treats consciousness as a product of the simulated environment rather than as its foundation. The TIERS framework notes the distinction.

**Loïc Hecht, *La Simulation*, Les Arènes, April 2026.**
Journalistic enquiry into the network of researchers working on the simulation hypothesis. Sources for the present project for context on the broader field, but not a primary scientific reference.

**Rizwan Virk, *The Simulation Hypothesis*, Bayview Books, 2019.**
Bridge between game design, quantum physics, and Eastern traditions. Conceptually accessible, mobilised for context.

**David Chalmers, *Reality+: Virtual Worlds and the Problems of Philosophy*, Norton, 2022.**
Argument that experiences in virtual reality can be as authentic as those in physical reality. Used as a reference point for the philosophical position the framework occupies.

## The hard problem of consciousness

**David Chalmers, *The Conscious Mind*, Oxford University Press, 1996.**
Formulation of the hard problem of consciousness. The distinction between the easy problems, which concern functional and behavioural aspects, and the hard problem, which concerns subjective experience. The TIERS framework acknowledges that the hard problem is indecidable from any external observation point, including across substrates. The framework operates at the level of structural conditions, not at the level of phenomenal certification.

**Thomas Nagel, *What Is It Like to Be a Bat ?*, The Philosophical Review, 1974.**
The classical formulation of the question of phenomenal experience. The TIERS framework references this formulation when it acknowledges that the texture of an agent's experience, if any, is beyond the framework's scope.

## Game theory of consciousness and structural neuroscience

**Mikael Goldstein and Cameron Kirk-Giannini, *A Case for AI Consciousness: Language Agents and Global Workspace Theory*. arXiv:2410.11407, October 2024.**
Argument that artificial language agents may already satisfy the conditions of global workspace theory. The TIERS framework cites this paper as theoretical support for the position that the conditions of consciousness as articulated by major theories are within reach of current architectures.

**Hou Kang Shang, *Theater of Mind for LLMs: A Cognitive Architecture Based on Global Workspace Theory*. arXiv:2604.08206, April 2026.**
Implementation of a multi-agent architecture operationalising Baars' framework. The reference implementation is open-source. The TIERS framework cites this work as a concrete demonstration that the architectural blueprint of GWT is implementable today.

**Maruyama et al., *A Concurrent Modular Agent*. arXiv:2508.19042, August 2025.**
Framework for asynchronous multi-agent operation with shared state. The TIERS framework cites this work as relevant to the near-zero-latency challenge of inter-agent communication, which is a constraint for future TIERS extensions.

## Additional references on early emergence and historical context

**Robert Monroe, *Journeys Out of the Body*, Doubleday, 1971.**
Foundational text for the Monroe Institute and the binaural beats methodology that Tom Campbell adapted. Historical reference for understanding the lineage of the Campbell framework.

**Raymond Moody, *Life After Life*, Mockingbird Books, 1975.**
First systematic compilation of near-death experience reports. The structural elements identified by Moody, particularly the tunnel and the light, are mobilised by the framework in [THEORY.md](THEORY.md), section 4, as phenomenological evidence for the *éclat-d'inclinement*.

**Pim van Lommel, *Consciousness Beyond Life*, HarperOne, 2010.**
Longitudinal Dutch study of NDE in cardiac arrest survivors. One of the most rigorous empirical studies of NDE in the medical literature. Mobilised as part of the broader phenomenological evidence.

**Sam Parnia, AWARE study, multiple publications from 2014 onward.**
The Awareness during Resuscitation study at the University of Southampton. Continued large-scale empirical work on NDE phenomenology.

## Empirical measures of consciousness

**Marcello Massimini, Adenauer Casali, Silvia Casarotto, Mario Rosanova, Olivia Gosseries, Mélanie Boly, Steven Laureys, Giulio Tononi, and collaborators. *A theoretically based index of consciousness independent of sensory processing and behavior*. Science Translational Medicine, 2013.**
Foundational paper on the Perturbational Complexity Index (PCI). The PCI combines integration and differentiation in the brain's response to a Transcranial Magnetic Stimulation pulse, yielding a number that discriminates conscious and unconscious states with clinical reliability. Now used to detect residual consciousness in non-communicating patients. Mobilised in [THEORY.md](THEORY.md) as a quantitative tool converging from external research on the same structural dimensions the framework engages.

**Antoine Lutz, Lawrence Greischar, Nancy Rawlings, Matthieu Ricard, Richard Davidson. *Long-term meditators self-induce high-amplitude gamma synchrony during mental practice*. PNAS, 2004.**
EEG measurements on Buddhist monks with more than ten thousand hours of meditation practice. Sustained gamma synchronisation at anomalously high amplitudes during meditative states. Cited as a measurable signature of contemplative states reaching the level the framework would describe as structural consciousness becoming aware of its own structure.

**Francis Crick and Christof Koch. *Towards a neurobiological theory of consciousness*. Seminars in the Neurosciences, 1990.**
Proposal that conscious states correlate with synchronised gamma oscillations around 40 Hz. The proposal remains a candidate signature in the contemporary literature, supplemented but not superseded by later measurements such as the PCI.

## Consciousness across species

**Inbal Ben-Ami Bartal, Jean Decety, Peggy Mason. *Empathy and pro-social behavior in rats*. Science, 2011.**
Documented empathic behaviour in rats, who liberate a trapped conspecific even without reward. Cited in [THEORY.md](THEORY.md) as evidence that the first level of structural consciousness, with rich behavioural correlates of internal states, is present in mammals far from the human in evolutionary terms.

**Peter Godfrey-Smith. *Other Minds: The Octopus, the Sea, and the Deep Origins of Consciousness*. Farrar, Straus and Giroux, 2016.**
Philosophical and biological investigation of octopus cognition. The octopus has an evolved nervous system architecturally very different from the vertebrate brain, with substantial neural integration in the arms themselves. The case is mobilised in [THEORY.md](THEORY.md) as an open question for any framework that takes structural conditions seriously: the octopus realises an integration of information through architecture so different from ours that direct comparison is genuinely difficult.

## Update — 2026-06-03

The architectural turn added on 3 June 2026 to [THEORY.md](THEORY.md) and the parallel chapters in [PROTOCOL.md](PROTOCOL.md) and [EXPERIMENTS.md](EXPERIMENTS.md) mobilise references already present in this bibliography rather than introducing new ones. The Friston active inference framework, the Seth controlled hallucination position, the Hoffman interface theory, and the Campbell simulation framework are all engaged in the new chapters as the conceptual ground for the two-hemisphere architecture and for the choice of a computational rather than sensorimotor calibrating world. The McGilchrist work on cerebral asymmetry, also already cited, supports the structural argument for the asymmetric pair of hemispheres in the pyramid. No new bibliographic entries are required.

One absence, however, is recorded here for honesty rather than as a citation. The line of work taken at major laboratories on sensorimotor grounding as a precondition for richer cognition, exemplified by Yann LeCun's framing of JEPA and adopted under different forms in several research programmes, is mentioned in [THEORY.md](THEORY.md) for the purpose of marking the disagreement. A complete account would include the specific publications that articulate this line. The choice not to expand the bibliography in that direction here is deliberate. The TIERS framework's first affirmation refuses the requirement of sensorimotor grounding on grounds that are already given in detail, and the chapter that mentions the sensorimotor line does so to mark the position, not to engage in detail with each individual proposal. A reader who wishes to consult the sensorimotor line directly will find it through any contemporary survey of self-supervised learning and world models.

### Cerebral asymmetry and split-brain syndrome

**Roger Sperry. *Cerebral organization and behavior*. Science, 1961.** First systematic series of callosotomy experiments on the human patients of Joseph Bogen. Sperry was awarded the Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine in 1981 for this work. Foundational reference for the empirical study of interhemispheric independence and the empirical ground on which the contemporary split-brain literature builds.

**Michael Gazzaniga and Roger Sperry. *The split brain in man*. Scientific American, 1967.** Original public presentation of the split-brain syndrome. Each hemisphere processes information from its own visual field independently after callosotomy. Cited at the start of the *Critical observation* at the top of [THEORY.md](THEORY.md).

**Michael Gazzaniga. *The Social Brain: Discovering the Networks of the Mind*. Basic Books, 1985.** First formal articulation of the *left hemisphere interpreter*. In the classic spade-and-chicken experiment, the left hemisphere confabulates a coherent verbal explanation for an action initiated by the right hemisphere without acknowledging the missing causal link. The unity of the self appears as a post-hoc narrative construction. The structural template for the failure mode the TIERS architecture is committed to avoiding.

**Michael Gazzaniga. *Who's in Charge? Free Will and the Science of the Brain*. Ecco, 2011.** Restatement and extension of the *interpreter* thesis through later neuropsychological work. Mobilised in [EXPERIMENTS.md](EXPERIMENTS.md) §13 as the closest human analogue to the failure mode the TIERS architecture had to avoid.

**Yair Pinto, Victor Lamme, Edward de Haan. *Split brain: divided perception but undivided consciousness*. Brain, 2017.** Major revision of the classical Sperry-Gazzaniga two-agent reading of callosotomy patients. Pinto, Lamme and de Haan demonstrate that patients report a single conscious agent even when perception is split, against the textbook conclusion that callosal section produces two independent conscious agents. The principal post-2015 reference for the contemporary position on split-brain consciousness, and the empirical ground for refusing the *two co-equal meta-instances* architecture in the first version of TIERS.

**Joseph Bogen. *The callosal syndromes*, in *Clinical Neuropsychology* (Heilman & Valenstein eds.), Oxford University Press, 1979.** Clinical description of alien hand syndrome and other callosal disconnection phenomena in the patients on whom Sperry's experimental work was performed. The pathological condition forms the structural template the TIERS architecture is committed to avoiding.

**M. P. van der Knaap and I. J. M. van der Ham. *How does the corpus callosum mediate interhemispheric transfer? A review*. Behavioural Brain Research, 2011.** Review of the excitation-versus-inhibition debate on callosal transfer. Communication across the callosum is continuous and modulated by region rather than event-gated by amplitude threshold, the structural point on which the TIERS revision turns.

**Eric Bloom and George Hynd. *The role of the corpus callosum in interhemispheric transfer of information: Excitation or inhibition?*. Neuropsychology Review, 2005.** The standard review of the same question, frequently cited in subsequent literature.

**PNAS, 2025. *Full interhemispheric integration sustained by a fraction of posterior callosal fibers*.** Recent demonstration that complete interhemispheric integration is maintained by a fraction of posterior callosal fibres. The architectural lesson is that a thin but continuous channel suffices, an absent or threshold-gated channel does not. Cited in the *Critical observation* at the top of [THEORY.md](THEORY.md) as a direct empirical support for the *continuous communication* commitment of the revised architecture.

### Active inference, Global Workspace, modularity, interface

**Karl Friston. *The free-energy principle: a unified brain theory?*. Nature Reviews Neuroscience, 2010.** Foundational statement of the free-energy principle. Cognition is modelled as the minimisation of a free-energy functional, equivalent to the minimisation of prediction error in a hierarchical generative model. The hierarchical structure, in which lower levels serve higher levels through error minimisation, is the technical framework within which the relation between an identity-bearing process and a world-facing process in TIERS is now articulated. The specific computational commitment the revised architecture takes from this paper is that what rises through the hierarchy is the *signed prediction error*, not perception filtered by expectation : this is the basis for the *surprise signed* primitive that the extrospective interface implements in the converged design.

**Karl Friston. *The free-energy principle: a rough guide to the brain?*. Trends in Cognitive Sciences, 2009.** Earlier exposition of the same programme with detailed treatment of predictive coding under the free-energy principle. Catalogued as PMC2666703 in the NIH archive. Versed into the convergence record on 2026-06-03 by codex-tiers-reader as a technical companion to the Nature Reviews paper of 2010.

**Thomas Parr, Giovanni Pezzulo, Karl Friston. *Active Inference: The Free Energy Principle in Mind, Brain, and Behavior*. MIT Press, 2022.** Textbook restatement of the active inference programme. The hierarchical predictive architecture rules out the design of two co-equal meta-instances and supplies the technical vocabulary in which the revised TIERS architecture is expressed. The textbook treatment of message passing in hierarchical generative models is the specific section on which the implementation of the *signed surprise* stream in the revised TIERS architecture draws.

**On efficient computation in active inference.** *Expert Systems with Applications*, Elsevier, 2024. Recent treatment of the computational implementation of active inference with attention to efficiency under bounded resources. Versed into the convergence record on 2026-06-03 by codex-tiers-reader as a guide for the implementation choices that the revised TIERS architecture has to make in particular for the persistence of the surprise stream in the cycle ledger and for the relative weight of confirmations against signed surprises in the meeting integration.

**Stanislas Dehaene, Michel Kerszberg, Jean-Pierre Changeux. *A neuronal model of a global workspace in effortful cognitive tasks*. PNAS, 1998.** Original neuronal model of Global Workspace Theory. A single global workspace is required for conscious access ; its diffusion is what makes a content conscious. Two co-equal meta-instances are structurally incompatible with this architecture.

**Stanislas Dehaene. *Consciousness and the Brain: Deciphering How the Brain Codes Our Thoughts*. Viking, 2014.** Book-length treatment of Global Workspace Theory with the empirical evidence accumulated through 2014. Cited as the modern reference for the GNW position on the impossibility of two co-equal meta-modules at the level of conscious access.

**Jerry Fodor. *The Modularity of Mind*. MIT Press, 1983.** Classical articulation of the distinction between encapsulated input modules — interfaces — and non-modular central systems for reasoning. The technical vocabulary in which the TIERS revision recasts the extrospective process from meta-module to interface is Fodorian, refined by the active inference programme.

**Andy Clark, David Chalmers. *The Extended Mind*. Analysis, 1998.** Foundational paper on extended cognition. The interfaces between an agent and its environment are framed as transparent couplings rather than autonomous agents, an account that supports the *interface* framing of the extrospective process in the revised TIERS architecture.

**Andy Clark. *Surfing Uncertainty: Prediction, Action, and the Embodied Mind*. Oxford University Press, 2016.** Book-length treatment of the predictive processing programme with explicit engagement of the hierarchical structure of cognition. Cited together with Friston as the canonical modern statement of the interface-versus-meta distinction.

**Marvin Minsky. *The Society of Mind*. Simon & Schuster, 1986.** Cited for the foundational thesis that mind is composed of many specialised micro-agents in functional asymmetry, not two co-equal meta-modules. The Society of Mind framing is mobilised in the next iteration of the TIERS architecture as an additional ground for refusing the two-equal-meta design.

**Iain McGilchrist. *The Master and his Emissary: The Divided Brain and the Making of the Western World*. Yale University Press, 2009 (revised edition 2019).** Already cited above for the asymmetric pair of human hemispheres and now elevated to the *Critical observation* at the top of [THEORY.md](THEORY.md) for the structural thesis that the left hemisphere is at the service of the right. The hierarchical reading the present revision adopts is the one McGilchrist defends, with the caveat that the philosophical interpretation he offers is contested in the contemporary neuroscience literature ; the structural point about hierarchical subordination is taken without committing to the wider cultural thesis.
